"Oppenheimer" follows J. Robert Oppenheimer, the physicist who led the Manhattan Project to develop the atomic bomb during World War II. The film explores the scientific and personal complexities of his life, from his early genius to his leadership of the project, the Trinity Test, and the ethical dilemmas he faced.

It depicts the intense pressure, secrecy, and moral quandaries of the project, as well as the political fallout and the profound impact of the atomic bomb on the world.
